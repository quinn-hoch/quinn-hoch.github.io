Quinn's Take on John Berger

I had seen a lot of these videos before, but not the second episode in which Berger discusses the perception and experience of women, and I really loved it. I appreciated how he didn't just spout what he thought women might think as a man, but included a panel of women to validate his beliefs—here is why women feel objectified, the systemic building of a society with certain focuses, and here is the proof that women do indeed feel this way—they'll articulate it themselves. Yet, this type of all-consumption is not limited to women. In advertising, we are driven to compare ourselves and our experiences to whatever it is we are viewing. To take a picture—buy or have a picture or painting or photo or reproduction—is to own some small part of it; to claim it as one's own. To own something is to be envied-or at least, to seek to be envied. And, to be envied is to be in the ultimate sense of the word, "glamourous". If there is one thing to take from Berger, it is the constant state of persuing "glamour" that we all inhabit. As Berger states—we cannot live without it.
