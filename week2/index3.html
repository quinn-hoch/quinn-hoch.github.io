Free the Internet!

...maybe?

Thoughts from Week 2 by Quinn Hochglaube

In "Long Live the Web" Tim Berners-Lee preaches that the internet must remain uncensored if it is to remain for the people -- no caveats, no exceptions. This struck me as very Kantian in its rigidity. If the internet is to remain an open resource for the people, should we not try to eliminate hate speech? What about misinformation? I understand the concept that people to some extent must figure out what they believe for themselves -- and to do so must be exposed to a variety of information and media -- but particularly in an era of unchecked AI and social unrest it feels like something must be done. In response to this, I think the Mozilla Manifesto advocates for a respectful and productive environment. However, I noticed there wasn't really an action plan even though there is obviously a problem. I get it -- it's difficult, I make no claim to have concrete ideas or solutions, but to improve we must start somewhere.
