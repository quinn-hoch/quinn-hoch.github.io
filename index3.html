Free the Internet!

...maybe?

In "Long Live the Web" Tim Berners-Lee preaches that the internet must remain uncensored if it is to remain for the people -- no caveats, no exceptions. This struck me as very Kantian because if the internet is to remain an open resource for the people, should we not try to eliminate hate speech? What about misinformation? I understand the concept that people to some extent must figure out what they believe for themselves -- and to do so must be exposed to a variety of information and media -- but particularly in an era of unchecked AI and social unrest it feels like something must be done. In response to this, I think the Mozilla Manifesto advocates for a respectful and productive environment. However, I noticed there wasn't really an action plan. I get it -- it's difficult, I have no concrete ideas, but to improve we must start somewhere.
